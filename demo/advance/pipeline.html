      PSEG joins $1 billion natural gas pipeline project to travel through Hopewell Valley

      (Patti Sapone/The Star Ledger )
      Print
      By Brendan McGrath | Times of Trenton The Times, Trenton
      Email the author | Follow on Twitter
      on September 25, 2014 at 5:40 PM, updated September 25, 2014 at 5:43 PM

      More Hopewell Valley News

      • Fine Arts: Local artists featured at Hopewell Valley Community Bank

      PSEG has joined the $1 billion project that is expected to bring a 30-inch wide natural gas pipeline from northeastern Pennsylvania down through Hunterdon County and into Hopewell Valley.

      The PennEast pipeline, which is currently projected to be in service in late 2017, will transport enough natural gas each to serve about 4.7 million homes, PennEast officials said.

      “Primarily the reason we did it was to increase the amount of gas that would be available in New Jersey,” Jennings said.

      As the demand for natural gas in the state continues to rise, the pipeline will allow the utility to meet the growing demand for natural gas, Jennings said. This will also allow PSEG to meet the part of the state’s energy master plan that calls for greater use of natural gas, Jennings said.

      Joining the project will also allow PSEG to obtain more of the lower priced gas from the Marcellus shale region, which the pipeline is tapping into in Pennsylvania, he said. Much of the current supply of gas that PSEG uses comes at a higher price from the Gulf region, he said.

      “This pipeline will provide much greater access to lower priced gas,” he said.

      PSEG Power LLC, a subsidiary of PSEG, will take a 12 percent interest in the PennEast venture, while the four other companies involved in the conglomerate, AGL Resources, NJR Pipeline, South Jersey Industries and UGI Energy Services, will each retain a 22 percent interest, PennEast officials said.

      “Now, nearly all major market centers in New Jersey will benefit from this direct connection to reliable and affordable Marcellus Shale gas from Pennsylvania,” said Peter Terranova, chair of the PennEast board of managers, in a statement.

      The pipeline proposal has raised concerns throughout Hopewell Valley and Hunterdon County as residents, municipalities and environmentalists question what the project will mean for their respective interests.
