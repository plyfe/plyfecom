      New procedures for neck, tailbone injuries remove pain, restore mobility

      Artificial disc replacement (ADR), one of the most advanced and successful treatments for neck pain (cervicalgia) caused by herniated discs, is performed as an outpatient procedure and offers advantages to traditional neck fusion surgery.
      Print Email
      Special to NJ.com By Special to NJ.com The Star-Ledger
      on March 26, 2015 at 5:00 AM, updated March 26, 2015 at 8:00 AM

      Dr. Scott Katzman, a board certified orthopedic surgeon and Tenafly native who has lectured and educated other physicians and companies on his innovative techniques, specializes in minimally invasive spinal and laser surgical procedures.

      A physician with New Jersey Spine and Orthopedic, he recently spoke with NJ Advance Media about the new techniques used in the practice's treatment for neck and tailbone pain.

      Artificial disc replacement (ADR), one of the most advanced and successful treatments for neck pain (cervicalgia) caused by herniated discs, is performed as an outpatient procedure and offers advantages to traditional neck fusion surgery.

      Another outpatient procedure, coccygectomy, safely alleviates prolonged or permanent pain from fractures of the coccyx (tailbone).

      Dr. Scott Katzman, board certified orthopedic surgeon specializing in outpatient spinal surgeries, NJ Spine and Orthopedic. Special to NJ.com 


      Q: What is a primary advantage of ADR to neck fusion surgery?
         A: In neck fusion, the hardware that is used to lock your neck stiff can lead to a new problem: adjacent segment disease. When neck motion in one area is removed, the remaining vertebrae must move more and work harder than was originally intended -- causing the bony structure to prematurely wear out. Neck-fusion patients are going back to the doctor every two to three years. This condition is not a factor with ADR. Our success rate is more than 95 percent.

      Q: What is the general age range of patients who are candidates for ADR?
         A: Most are under age 60. The younger a patient is, the more likely it is that someone is going to offer treatment for herniated discs. The No. 1 reason for this condition is trauma, whether it's from playing football, a car accident or a fall. ADR gives patients a more fulfilling, active lifestyle. They will have renewed, full motion to play sports, participate in outdoor activities, etc. Golfers like ADR because it restores that motion of turning the head, enabling them to see the ball.

      Q: What other benefits does ADR offer?
         A: It's a new technology, but 15-year studies now are showing that artificial disc replacement is three times better than a fusion -- it lasts longer, offers a quicker recovery time, requires less surgeries, and results in less stiffness and revision.

      It's so much better on every level. ADR is FDA-approved and we offer a free MRI evaluation.

      Q: Explain the debilitating pain of a tailbone fracture and how it can be alleviated.
         A: The coccyx is essentially a residual tail and due to a fall, can sustain a fracture, which often does not heal properly due to scant blood supply. A broken tailbone causes pain during sitting, walking and other daily activities. Approximately 80 percent of patients are women. Some patients do not respond to traditional treatments, which include a conservative approach, lifestyle changes and physical therapy. For these patients, removal of the broken tailbone -- which, like the tonsils, is not needed -- can offer immediate relief.

      Q: Is the procedure safe?
         A: There's a misconception that surgery involving the coccyx is dangerous, because of its location and that an infection could result. That is not my experience. I do very small incisions. I'm willing to say that I've done more of these surgeries than anyone in the United States. Patients who come to see me have been offered injections, but they have chronic pain and become very hopeless. When they visit me, they say, 'How come no one else does this?' We have a 99 percent success rate. I love to do this procedure and help people.

      Learn more at njspineandortho.com.
