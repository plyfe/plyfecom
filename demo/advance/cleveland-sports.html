      Can Cleveland Browns' rookie QB Johnny Manziel count on Josh Gordon?

      Cleveland Browns vs Indianapolis Colts
      Cleveland Browns wide receiver Josh Gordon misses a deep pass along the sidelines from Brian Hoyer late in the Browns' loss to the Indianapolis Colts. Will Gordon and Johnny Manziel necessarily have better chemistry in the rookie's first start? (John Kuntz)
      Print
      Bud Shaw, The Plain Dealer By Bud Shaw, The Plain Dealer The Plain Dealer
      Follow on Twitter
      on December 12, 2014 at 11:00 AM, updated December 12, 2014 at 11:08 AM
      View/Post Comments

      CLEVELAND, Ohio – Because there's always something to spin in Cleveland sports...

      • Josh Gordon says it's ridiculous to think he will play harder for Johnny Manziel than he did Brian Hoyer.

      "It's like trying to throw a game on purpose. Nobody wants to do that -- no matter if you like a guy or you don't like a guy," Gordon told reporters. "I love Brian. He's been here since I first got here just about.

      "He was my quarterback last year. He's a competitor, I had nothing wrong with him, and I never even played with Johnny before -- so that's crazy to think that.''

      So if I read him correctly he's saying he will play equally hard, or not, for both guys.

      • Mike Pettine doesn't believe Gordon's conditioning is a problem.

      "Sometimes it's confidence and not conditioning – knowing exactly what to do," said the Browns' head coach said.

      Feel better?

      • Here's what we can probably say about the Gordon-Manziel connection: We'll see if there's something to be said for two guys making it up as they go along. Together.

      • With the Bengals in town this weekend, we revisit Cincinnati running back Jeremy Hill's take after the Browns won, 24-3, last month.

      "They're probably worse than I thought to be honest with you," Hill said of the Browns. "They didn't do anything special to me."

      The Bengals won three of their next four.

      The Browns lost three of their next four.

      So while his timing was terrible, it's not like he called the Seahawks a Super Bowl pretender.

      Or insulted a kid quarterback minding his own business, and an entire group of people with one word.

      • A Sports Illustrated article on Tom Brady's obsessive regimen meant to keep him physically and mentally prepared for the grind of NFL seasons includes an anecdote involving former Patriots' safety Rodney Harrison.

      Seems first time Harrison showed up to lift weights at 6:40 a.m., Brady greeted him by saying, "Good afternoon."

      Next day, Harrison came in at 6:30, then 6:20. Each time, Brady said, "Good afternoon." Harrison decided he couldn't come in any earlier.

      No names, but if you have a NFL rookie quarterback on your Christmas list there's an idea: a subscription to SI.

      And an alarm clock.

      • There's lots of ways to be successful in the NFL. But one thing you don't want your quarterback saying to teammates at 6:40 in the morning?

      "Good night."

      • When Brady is asked how long he expects to play, his answer is now, "Forever."

      Or until Bill Belichick does a night at The Improv, whatever comes first.

      • Chicago Bears' offensive coordinator Aaron Kromer apologized to players for anonymously ripping quarterback Jay Cutler to the NFL.com for his decision making.

      It's not likely Bears' players readily accepted Kromer's apology.

      After all, they prefer to be the ones ripping Cutler anonymously.

      • Air Force football coach Troy Calhoun told the Colorado Springs Gazette the four-team college football playoff is "un-American" because it leaves little to no room for non-power conference teams.

      Air Force will play Western Michigan in the Famous Idaho Potato Bowl, not that Calhoun is complaining about that.

      "If we had to go to Siberia, OK, to play the Leningrad Stalins or the Leningrad Rooskies, we'd go do it," said Calhoun, who obviously doesn't see things in black-and-white as much as red-white-and-blue.


       
      Kyle Shanahan shares his view of the Browns offense with Johnny Manziel at the helm Cleveland Browns offensive coordinator Kyle Shanahan talks about what he expects out of rookie quarterback Johnny Manziel who will be making his first NFL start Sunday against the Cincinnati Bengals.

      • Kobe Bryant raised the tension level his first full Lakers' practice in two weeks by challenging teammates and calling them "soft like Charmin."

      So the Rooskie reference won't be the most dated one in this column today.

      • Lakers' guard Nick Young supposedly told Bryant, "Nobody in the world can guard me one-on-one."

      Bryant to reporters: "Thank God I'm not from this world."

      • In seven road games this season, Seattle's Russell Wilson has 15 TDs and one interception.

      For some crazy reason, no opposing coach has mentioned his height.

      • Nebraska defensive end Jack Gangwish took a selfie with an uncooperative roadside raccoon. He said he killed the raccoon with a crescent wrench after it bit him in the calf.

      "I'm sure y'all be excited to hear that the results came back and I do not have Rabies," he said on Twitter.

      Not really.

      You'd like to think he's kidding and couldn't possibly be so cruel and stupid.

      Because if not, put me down as rooting for the rabies.

      • If the Browns can win Sunday, they'd take an 8-6 record to Carolina to face the Panthers.

      It's too early to tell whether Cam Newton could play. He will miss Sunday's game after he was injured in a car accident earlier this week.

      That means Derek Anderson could keep the Browns out of the playoffs in the season's next-to-last game.

      Just like in 2007.

      • Wisconsin head coach Gary Anderson left the Badgers to take the Oregon State job.

      Apparently at halftime of the Ohio State game.

      • Yoenis Cespedes, recently acquired by the Detroit Tigers from the Red Sox, drives a half-million dollar Lamborghini Aventador that spits flames out the exhaust.

      No word on how he decided on the Lamborghini over his second choice, the Prius C, other than the obvious need to drive something that can be seen from space.

      For more Bud Shaw, read You Said It.
