Buying Guide to Beds

Go Back To Guides

When decorating a bedroom in your home, there’s no question that it will be the bed itself that draws the most attention. You may have a great bureau, super window coverings, and the perfect rug, but without a bed that matches your style and vision, your bedroom will become just another sleeping area. But with so many different bed styles out there, how can you know which one is right for you (and the size of your room)? Let’s take a look at the different variations and help you avoid any “sleepless nights” wondering about which bed to buy!

Here’s the quick low-down on some common bed styles. What appeals to you?

Style Facts
Four Posters A four-poster bed evokes the feeling of a strong, substantial piece of furniture. As the name suggests, a four-poster bed has a tall post at each corner (built into the head- and footboards). These posts may either terminate on their own or support an additional horizontal frame known as a tester that ties the four posts together. There are many styles available from sleek and trim to solid and bold.
Low Post When you try to conjure up an image of a basic, classic bed, you’re probably thinking of something like a basic low-post bed. This type has a low post on each of the four bed corners. There are a lot of good things to be said about these beds including their ability to blend in to just about any space and décor.
Sleigh While the exact look and feel can vary widely, sleigh beds are intended to give the suggestion of an old-fashioned horse-drawn sleigh, complete with curves and lines. You’ll discover both wooden and metal sleigh beds, with headboards and footboards of all heights — but in general, these tend to be fairly large beds. The big question here is if you find the curved look appealing, or if you’d rather go towards the traditional straight-line look of other designs.
Metal Metal beds can give a nice classic feel, while not taking up much space or drawing a lot of attention to themselves. Metal beds tend to have a curvier flair than other styles, but not always. You’ll find them in a range of styles, as well as a range of finishes and paint colors.
Platform Platform beds often have a stylish, modern, sleek, sophisticated feel. This is partly because their design allows them to support a mattress without any need for a box spring. This allows the platform bed to have a simpler, lower profile. You also have an opportunity to save a little money by foregoing the purchase of a box spring.
Panel Are similar to platform beds, but provide side rails to hold the box spring and mattress. Panel beds have a high headboard and a lower profile footboard and are generally made from wood. The head or footboard often feature wood carvings, panels or upholstery and become a beautiful focal point in the bedroom.
Upholstered As long as you don’t mind the possibility of a bit more upkeep, upholstered beds can create a vibrant, plush environment. Choose a fabric design with just the colors you’re looking for, or opt for vinyl or leather.
Canopy Looking for that romantic, day-dreamy, yet spacious feel? A canopy bed can deliver on all these specifications. It builds on the four-poster concept, but takes it further by utilizing a series of overhead frames capable of holding curtains. Therefore, a canopy bed has the potential to be a very striking element that can make a bedroom really stand out. It can have a traditional or contemporary feel, but be careful to keep your bedroom’s ceiling height and general size in mind — don’t buy a canopy that is too large, as it may overpower the room.
Daybed Daybeds have a lot of good points. A daybed is a twin-sized mattress supported by a sofa-like wood or metal frame. During the day, a daybed can be used much like a sofa (often with plenty of pillows handy!) for sitting around and hanging out, but has the option of converting into a usable bed at night. This situation makes daybeds great for guest rooms! They also work well in children’s rooms and small apartments by serving dual purposes, thus saving both space and money.

Measurements – Select the size that will accommodate your mattress.

Twin 39" x 75"
Full 54" x 75"
Queen 60" x 80"
King 76" x 80"
California King 72" x 84"

It’s quite important to make sure you don’t “overbuy”— that is, that you don’t purchase a bed that is too large for the room. While you want the bed to (naturally) be the focal point of the bedroom, it shouldn’t be the only thing in it, creating tight squeezes and leaving no room for other furniture. Likewise, you probably don’t want to go too small, but this only becomes an issue in very large rooms. You might find it helpful to measure the width and length of the room ahead of time and then visualize — or perhaps even make a scaled sketch of — the room with your intended bed size in mind. Remember: Empty or nearly-empty rooms seem much bigger than they really are, so it’s easy to assume you have more room to work with than you do, so measure, measure, measure! Don’t forget to consider the frame style along with head- and footboards as well, as these take up additional space. Also, figure on leaving about 24 inches between the sides of your bed and any walls — you’ll appreciate the space when working around the bed and moving about. Likewise, if you’re planning a children’s room with two beds, you’ll want at least two feet — probably more — between them.

Also, consider any pre-existing decor in the room. Is your new bed going to blend well with the other furniture? What about the walls, wallpaper, window coverings, etc.? Consider bringing a photo of the room with you while shopping to help you select a style that works (Does this silvery metal bed go with the decor? What about this deep mahogany?) Of course, you can always work the other way around and design the rest of the room to match your perfect bed — whatever works to help you achieve your goals and (sweet) dreams!
