Judge Orders Bethenny Frankel to Grow Up and Out of Her Daughter’s PJs

by Meredith Carroll | Posted 5 days ago
FacebookTwitterGoogle+TumblrPinterest

Google “Bethenny Frankel pajamas” and the search will yield 301,000 results (including a piece I wrote).

In other words, there’s been no shortage of words, discussion, and debate over whether it was wildly inappropriate or just plain cute a few months ago when the SkinnyGirl mogul, former talk show host, and reality TV starlet posted a photo of herself on Instagram wearing her 4-year-old daughter’s Hello Kitty pajamas.

Some people thought it was no big deal and others said if they could fit into a Size 4T, they would do it, too. Others still — including me — thought it was weird on a professional level (yes, there is such thing as bad publicity) as well as a personal level. I said, in part:

She’s savvy, shrewd, and knows her brand, which is her. At some point, though, she’s going to need to take a long, hard look in the mirror and realize that while she is, indeed, a brand, she’s also a mom.

It wouldn’t be fair to take a single snapshot in a day of Bethenny’s life to extrapolate that she’s putting career above family (which plenty of men and women do).

But it seems to me that when you’re a businesswoman in the spotlight and a mom, you should maybe choose more carefully the images of you that you want to put out there for public consumption — on behalf of your customers as well as your children. Infantalizing yourself in your child’s pajamas — because you’re skinny enough to wear them — seems to me as if it’s sending out more than one message, the majority of which are not eminently positive.

If she wants to set a good example for her daughter and the thousands of women who helped put her where she is today, do it by eating for health reasons (not not eating to stay skinny), and to respect herself and her daughter enough by keeping some things in the family, or better yet, not doing them at all.

Bethenny responded to her critics at the time on by tweeting, “When ur 4 year old peanut says ‘mommy please put my dress on’ & giggles uncontrollably, u do what ur told.”

However, what she’s being told now is putting an end to any semblance of a discussion. According to the New York Daily News, Bethenny has been ordered by a judge to stop wearing her daughters PJs.

Manhattan Supreme Court Justice Ellen Gesmer told Bethenny’s lawyers on Thursday the image will last online for so long that her daughter will eventually be able to find it, as well as what has been said about it, on her own.

“It’s not a joke. Her child is not a joke,” Gesmer said.

You could argue it’s strange that a judge weighed in on the photo since the custody battle between Bethenny and estranged husband Jason Hoppy is settled — they’ll share parenting responsibilities equally. The hearing where the judge ordered Bethenny to stop wearing little-girl pajamas for the world to see was part of the ex-couple’s divorce proceedings.

You could also argue people like me — and the rest of the Internet — should not have weighed in on Pajamagate, either. But the fact is that Bethenny put it out there, she’s a very public figure by choice, and while I judged her (along with some 301,000 other sites, for better or worse), an actual judge just shut it down from happening again — publicly at least.

Reality stars, bonafide Hollywood stars, writers, and real people can talk and over-share, and talk about over-sharing until the cows come home, but I don’t think I’m alone in finding an actual judge judging one of these much-talked about photos a pretty interesting thing. All of us judge each other in some way, on some level, pretty much all the time. Some of us do it for a living, while others put themselves out there to be judged for a living.

That an actual judge stepped in and judged a businesswoman and mom, telling her to stop putting herself out there in that kind of way so her daughter won’t judge her one day, speaks volumes. For the same reason as the judge’s reason, I hope Bethenny will really hear it.
