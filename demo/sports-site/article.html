    Horace Grant: Michael Jordan’s Bulls were better than LeBron James’ Heat

    NBA
    By Bob White

    Horace Grant, who was with Michael Jordan’s Bulls during their first three-peat from 1991-93, doesn’t think LeBron James’ Heat could hang with his Chicago teams.

    “The Heat wouldn’t have had a chance,” Grant said Tuesday on WSCR-AM in Chicago, according to ESPNChicago.com. “We would have locked them up. We would have locked them up. Especially with the rules today, Michael would have had a field day.”

    The Heat are aiming for a new championship this year.

    Miami is looking to play its fifth straight finals, which Jordan’s Bulls never did. Jordan took a hiatus to play baseball after the first three-peat and didn’t return until late in the 1994-95 season.

    Grant, 48, is pulling for any team that plays against Miami, of course, he is defending his legacy.

    “My fingers and toes are crossed that Miami's rival wins just for the fact of wanting to maintain my legacy and Michael”s team.”

    Grant won three titles with Chicago and another with the Lakers in 2001.

    In what concerns to the Heat, defense is the foundation of their success. It is the team“s alpha and omega. But Miami did not look anything like the two-time defending champion last game at AmericanAirlines Arena. In fact, judging by the disgusted look on Pat Riley“s face, the Heat was an offending defending champion.

    There“s a lot they need to do if they expect to win another ring. Unless the Heat can unlock its DNA on D, they wont make it to the new championship and any fading hope of a three-peat will be snuffed in Miami on Sunday. For the second game in a row, the Heat helped its rival see the hoop as a magnified target — as wide as Biscayne Bay. After yielding a 59.4 percent shooting percentage in last game“s loss, Miami again failed to clamp down, allowing the opposite team to capitalize on 57.1 percent accuracy to take a 3-1 lead in the best-of-seven series.
    This is not over yet, we“ll see if they can twist this story once again.
